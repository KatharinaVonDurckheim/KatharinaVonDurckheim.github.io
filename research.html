Research

Understanding how animals move through changing landscapes

My research integrates telemetry, environmental data and quantitative modelling to address ecological and conservation questions.

Elephant movement ecology

Space use, behavioural states, habitat selection, water dependence, anthropogenic pressure and movement across protected-area boundaries in southern African transfrontier conservation areas.

Lion ecology and landscape risk

Home ranges, habitat selection, movement responses to anthropogenic disturbance and potential changes between historical and contemporary landscapes.

Thermal refugia and climate adaptation

Identifying fine-scale thermal refugia and understanding how large mammals modify movement and behaviour under extreme heat.

Connectivity and conservation planning

Translating movement data into evidence for corridors, permeability, protected-area planning and transboundary conservation policy.
